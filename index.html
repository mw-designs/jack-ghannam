ResearchGate - NIH ResearchGate - Loyola

Apply genomic and cancer biology approaches to define the molecular taxonomy and tumor microenvironment of skull base tumors. Further developing my understanding of tumor cell evolution and their acquisition of resistance to innate immune mechanisms as well as administered treatments. Define molecular markers with diagnostic and prognostic potential to track brain tumors across time and to develop improved therapeutic options.

  • My current rotation project with Dr. Lind Bi and Dr. Rameen Beroukhim entails studying the genomic drivers and microenvironment of meningioma and other CNS tumors (e.g., glioma). My primary project has been the implementation and application of a novel algorithm to a cohort of over 1000 meningioma microarray samples to identify new drivers of tumorigenesis, in addition to evaluating the selective pressure driving aneuploidy in meningioma
  • Secondary projects include interrogating the role of a TERT promotor mutation in the recurrence of meningioma, and in particular recurrence of meningioma into higher grade tumors. This entails wet-lab work, and ultimately the sequencing and analysis of 200-500 meningioma samples
  • Tertiary projects include collaborations with the David A. Barbie to leverage their organoid system to assess meningioma immune microenvironment, and in particular the role of innate immune system cells in tumorigenesis in addition to the impact that STING agonists could play in potential treatment
  • Assist master’s student in the wet-lab component of thesis project
  • Learned the fundamentals of how to use the R programming language for purposes of biomedical research
