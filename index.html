ResearchGate - NIH ResearchGate - Loyola

BWH, DFCI and the Broad Institute, MD-PhD Rotation Student | Boston, MA

Apply genomic and cancer biology approaches to define the molecular taxonomy and tumor microenvironment of skull base tumors. Further developing my understanding of tumor cell evolution and their acquisition of resistance to innate immune mechanisms as well as administered treatments. Define molecular markers with diagnostic and prognostic potential to track brain tumors across time and to develop improved therapeutic options.

  • My current rotation project with Dr. Lind Bi and Dr. Rameen Beroukhim entails studying the genomic drivers and microenvironment of meningioma and other CNS tumors (e.g., glioma). My primary project has been the implementation and application of a novel algorithm to a cohort of over 1000 meningioma microarray samples to identify new drivers of tumorigenesis, in addition to evaluating the selective pressure driving aneuploidy in meningioma
  • Secondary projects include interrogating the role of a TERT promotor mutation in the recurrence of meningioma, and in particular recurrence of meningioma into higher grade tumors. This entails wet-lab work, and ultimately the sequencing and analysis of 200-500 meningioma samples
  • Tertiary projects include collaborations with the David A. Barbie to leverage their organoid system to assess meningioma immune microenvironment, and in particular the role of innate immune system cells in tumorigenesis in addition to the impact that STING agonists could play in potential treatment
  • Assist master’s student in the wet-lab component of thesis project
  • Learned the fundamentals of how to use the R programming language for purposes of biomedical research

Mass General Hospital, MD-PhD Rotation Student | Boston, MA

Perform translation genomics to identify drivers of progression in human brain tumors, predominantly brain tumor metastases, functionally characterize the candidate drivers in vitro and in vivo, and apply novel therapeutics (i.e., targeted therapy and immunotherapy) preclinically to be translated into clinical trial under the mentorship of Dr. Priscilla Brastianos of MGH’s Brain Tumor Research Center. Work includes, but not limited to, bulk NGS and single-cell sequencing, use of mouse models of brain tumors/metastasis, and sequencing and digital PCR for ctDNA detection in liquid biopsies.

  • Acquired skills in working with mice models to assess efficacy of novel therapies in a BSL-2 setting. This included engrafting subcutaneous, intrathoracic, and intracranial tumors into both immunocompetent and incompetent mice, and administering experimental therapies via intraperitoneal therapies and oral gavage
  • Headed lab effort to apply previously acquired skills in the use of digital droplet PCR (ddPCR) for sensitive detection of rare events to determine if circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) could be detected via ddPCR in mice models. Optimized workflow for cell-free DNA (cfDNA) extraction from low-volume mice plasma and developed and optimized ddPCR assays for the sensitive detection of mutations of interest
  • Headed lab effort to establish a tissue clearing protocol for the enhanced visualization of brain-slice optical imaging in mouse model cohorts. This entailed a thorough literature review to determine how the strengths and drawbacks of different protocols related to our specific hypotheses
  • Learned workflows and protocols for tissue acquisition and processing from operating rooms, clinic, and collaborators, in addition to the sample management system
  • Attended clinical meetings such as Brain Metastasis Tumor Board, which included a multidisciplinary dialogue to determine best treatment options for patient cases at MGH with brain metastases

Postbac Fellow | Bethesda, MD

Performed translational human immunology research under the guidance of Dr. Christopher Hourigan, discovering ways to detect, prevent and treat Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) and AML relapse. This included developing novel sequencing tools for the sensitive detection of residual disease in order to predict patients who may be at risk of relapse and whom may benefit from additional therapy.

  • Awarded Mission Bio grant challenge to develop and apply a novel, microfluidics-based, single-cell DNA sequencing technology in the context of relapsed/refractory AML. Developing and applying this technology was my primary project during my time at the NIH
  • Lead projects regarding application of single-cell DNA sequencing applications in AML, Sickle Cell Disease (SCD), and Aplastic Anemia (AA). Also lead projects exploring the etiology of AML development in SCD patients receiving gene therapy, and AA patients receiving BM transplant (see publications)
  • Awarded NIH's Orloff Science Award for "Work Demonstrating That Intervention for Acute Myeloid Leukemia Patients in Remission but With Genomic Evidence of Residual Disease Can Improve Survival"
  • Collaborated with biomedical companies directly (Qiagen, Eppendorf, 10x, Mission Bio, Invitrogen) to help develop and apply their products to our research (e.g., sequencing panels)
  • Developed wet lab protocols for Next Generation Sequence (NGS)-based residual disease detection. Streamlined workflow for bulk sequencing, including DNA isolation from patient whole blood to preparing patient’s DNA for NGS library preparation; lab skills include human sample processing from bone marrow and blood, operating automated liquid handlers, preparing bulk and single cell sequencing libraries, analyzing sequencing data, performing qPCR, ddPCR, and cell culturing
  • Observed surgeries and procedures while obtaining samples (e.g., neurosurgery and bone marrow transplant)
  • Attend classes in bioinformatics to analyze NGS and other data at the NIH’s graduate school (FAES) as well as presentations and conferences relevant to immunology and leukemia

Laboratory Assistant | Chicago, IL

Applied synthetic and physical inorganic chemistry to address current challenges in areas such as coordination chemistry, medicinal inorganic chemistry, bioinorganic chemistry, and renewable energy. Completed advanced training in synthesis via designing and preparing molecular complexes, and in physical methods of spectroscopy (IR, NMR, UV, Evans Method), crystallography, and voltammetry.

  • Developed and fully characterized 12 novel inorganic nickel and vanadium complexes and developed a nickel system that helped uncover the mechanism of C-H bond activation in the nickel-nitridyl milieu
  • Adept with operation and maintenance of nitrogen atmosphere glove box, wet glove box for anaerobic operation, solvent purification system, photoreactor, and gas addition apparatus
  • Taught incoming undergraduate and graduate students proper laboratory safety and fundamental techniques of organic and inorganic synthesis
  • Presented poster at Loyola Chemistry Department poster session, and at Loyola’s weekend of excellence
  • Published two 1st author publications in addition to a third paper as a co-author
  • Invited to submit cover art for Inorganic Chemistry Journal Volume 58, Issue 11
